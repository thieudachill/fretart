FretArt — choose ONE brand concept · Phase 5 · A, B, or C

Three complete directions, each generated + QC'd through the same process (prompts and attempt logs in ATTEMPTS.md). The winner becomes the brand for the whole project — app UI theme, landing page, README hero. Each concept is rooted in a palette the app already ships, so the brand always matches what the screen renders. Mixing is allowed (e.g. concept A's mark with another's palette) but one direction should lead.

Concept AStudio Ink — one continuous line

Quiet, gallery-like, drawn by a pen that never lifts. The finger flow itself is the brand. Strength: closest to the app's default look, ages well, reads "art project". Risk: understated — less punchy as a thumbnail.

More A: alternates, spots, hero loop, grain

Concept BPrint Shop — misregistered riso

Bold flat shapes, two or three ink layers printed slightly off — the app's own misreg parameter made into a brand. Strength: loud, instantly recognizable, great thumbnails and posters. Risk: warmer/retro mood than the app's muted default presets.

Concept CBlueprint — cyanotype of music

The hand as an engineering diagram: pale drafting lines, dimension marks, callout circles on Prussian blue. "The geometry of playing," which is literally what the app computes. Strength: unique, technical-poetic, dark-mode native. Risk: cooler/quieter emotionally; the app defaults to warm paper looks.

How to answer: just say "A", "B", or "C" (plus any mix-and-match notes). The winner's palette gets cut into src/ui/theme.css, its assets graduate to public/brand/, and Phases 6–8 build on it.
